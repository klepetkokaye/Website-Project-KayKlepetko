Conclusion

  Through this study of Dave and Dirk Strider, and how Andrew Hussie uses them to explore the essential nature of identity, one is able to understand that Hussie portrays identity as not just something stable, but something that is innate and unchangeable. He uses both Dave and Dirk to explore the idea that we are shaped and formed by the world we function in. He rejects this idea by having Dave reject the ideals that he was raised in and, through Dirk, he rejects the idea by maintaining that even if his doubles experience different things, and even have different memories, they are all the same person and no matter what makes Dirk happier, to deny that is to deny the truth. Through this one comes to understand that through Homestuck, Hussie communicates the idea that who we are is not due to the world around us, by the very nature of our souls, even if how we see ourselves is in contrast to that.
